Climbing Tips for New Adventurers

Climbing Nepal’s peaks or local crags is exhilarating but requires preparation. These tips will help beginners start safely and confidently.

1. Learn Basic Techniques

Start at a climbing gym to master foot placement, grip techniques, and body positioning. Focus on using legs to push rather than pulling with arms to save energy.

  • Practice smearing and edging for better footwork.
  • Learn to rest on holds to conserve strength.
  • Try bouldering to build confidence without ropes.

3. Climb with a Guide

Hire certified guides for Nepal’s peaks like Island Peak. They teach rope management, belaying, and safety protocols, ensuring a safe learning experience.

  • Look for AMGA or UIAGM-certified guides.
  • Join group clinics for cost-effective learning.

4. Build Physical Endurance

Climbing demands strength and stamina. Train with:

  • Cardio (running, cycling) for endurance.
  • Core exercises (planks, leg raises) for stability.
  • Fingerboard training for grip strength.

5. Respect the Environment

Follow Leave No Trace principles to protect climbing areas:

  • Avoid chalk overuse; clean holds after climbing.
  • Stick to established trails to prevent erosion.
  • Pack out all waste, including tape and wrappers.
